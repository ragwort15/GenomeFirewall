Literature & clinical research

paper-qa over 3 open-access PDF(s) for blaKPC-2.

The blaKPC-2 gene encodes a carbapenemase enzyme that hydrolyzes carbapenem antibiotics, leading to resistance in Klebsiella pneumoniae. This enzyme deactivates the antibiotic by breaking the β-lactam ring, a critical structure for the antibiotic's bactericidal activity (tuhamize2024carbapenemresistantenterobacteriaceaein pages 12-13). The blaKPC-2 gene's expression and copy number can increase under antibiotic stress, further enhancing resistance levels (hamza2025emergingresistanceto pages 23-24). This gene is primarily associated with resistance to β-lactam antibiotics, including carbapenems
